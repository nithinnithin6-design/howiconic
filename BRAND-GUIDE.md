## SECTION 0: THE PARIJATA STORY

### The Founding Flower

When HowIconic's founder built a three-floor office, he filled it with plants.

Every floor. Every corner. Green everywhere.

He stood in the middle of it all and felt — not quite satisfied. Something was missing. Not a plant he'd forgotten to place. Something harder to name.

A colleague mentioned the Parijata. *Nyctanthes arbor-tristis.* The night-flowering jasmine.

He placed one on the terrace.

The next morning, he found it waiting for him. White flowers on the ground — dozens of them, each with five petals pure as first light and a tiny stem the color of flame. An intoxicating fragrance that had filled the terrace while he slept. The tree had bloomed in the dark, quietly, without an audience. And then it had given everything away.

He stood there for a long time.

HowIconic was born from that morning.

---

### The Mythology

The Parijata carries stories older than memory.

In the *Samudra Manthan* — the churning of the cosmic ocean — the gods and asuras pulled a great serpent around the mountain Mandara and turned the ocean itself into a wheel of creation. From the depths rose fourteen treasures. Among them: the Parijata, the wish-fulfilling flower, gift of the cosmic churn. It was placed in Indra's heaven, where it bloomed eternally.

There is another story, quieter and more heartbreaking.

A princess fell in love with Surya, the Sun God. The sun cannot be held. He left. From her grief — from the ashes of her devotion — grew the tree that carries her name. The Parijata.

Look closely at the flower and you'll see the story written in it: the orange stem is the fire of her love, still burning. The white petals are her purity, untouched by rejection. The tree blooms only at night — because daylight belongs to the one who left her. It offers its flowers before dawn, before he rises again.

And here is the detail that separates the Parijata from every other flower in the world:

It falls from the tree before it dies. It offers itself to the ground — fresh, fragrant, complete.

The Parijata is the only flower in Hindu tradition that may be offered to the gods *after* it has fallen. You don't need to pluck it. It gives itself.

Its Sanskrit names hold the whole story:  
**Harsingar** — *ornament of the gods.*  
**Rajanī-hāsa** — *the night's smile.*

---

### What This Means for HowIconic

We build brands the way the Parijata blooms.

Not loudly. Not with announcements. With precision, care, and the quiet confidence that what is built well will be found in the morning — beautiful, complete, offered freely.

The orange that runs through everything we make is the Parijata's stem. The fire of devotion. The thing that persists after everything else has changed.

The white is the petal. Clean. Unadorned. The thing that carries the fragrance.

The dark canvas is the night in which all of this becomes possible.

HowIconic does not generate brands. It grows them. The founder's inputs are the soil. The seven-step process is the plant growing, root by root, branch by branch, toward something that will bloom. The final brand identity is the flower on the ground at dawn — yours to find, yours to keep.

**Build what lasts.**  
*Your brand, in full bloom.*

## SECTION 1: BRAND STRATEGY

### 1.1 Positioning Statement

HowIconic is a Brand Operating System for founders, designers, and brand managers who understand that their brand is a business asset — not a design deliverable.

We sit in the whitespace between a $35 logo generator and a $350,000 agency. We deliver the strategic rigour of a top-tier consultancy through a product that anyone can use.

**Who it's for:** Founders building something worth remembering. Designers who want a system, not just a canvas. Brand managers who need to move fast without cutting corners.

**Why it matters:** Most businesses never invest seriously in their brand. Either the cost is prohibitive (agency) or the quality is inadequate (DIY tool). HowIconic makes the case that there is no version of success without brand clarity — and then removes every obstacle to achieving it.

Like the Parijata, we don't wait to be discovered. We're there every morning.

---

### 1.2 Mission

Give every builder access to the brand thinking that used to cost $100,000.

---

### 1.3 Vision

Today: A Brand Operating System that architects, stores, and produces your brand identity.

Tomorrow: The Brand Command Center — a living intelligence layer that understands your brand as deeply as you do, enforces it everywhere it appears, and evolves it as you grow.

The long arc: every business in the world should have a brand that matches the quality of its ambition. HowIconic makes that possible.

Some flowers bloom in cultivated gardens. The Parijata grows on terraces, in courtyards, in the smallest patches of available soil. It doesn't ask for ideal conditions. It asks only for a chance.

HowIconic is the same. Wherever a founder is trying to build something real — that's our territory.

---

### 1.4 Core Values

**1. Strategy first.**  
We believe that a beautiful logo built on a weak foundation is just expensive decoration. Every HowIconic output starts with thinking. We ask harder questions than competitors because hard questions produce lasting answers. The Parijata doesn't bloom arbitrarily — it follows the rhythm of night and dawn, rooted in cycle and intention. So do we.

**2. Craft over output.**  
We grow brands. We don't generate content. There is a meaningful difference between a brand crafted with intention and a logo spat out by a template engine. We hold ourselves to the former, always. The Parijata takes a full night to bloom. We take the time the work deserves.

**3. Clarity is kindness.**  
We don't obscure or complicate. Plain language. Honest pricing. Transparent process. We respect our users' intelligence and their time. The Parijata doesn't hold its fragrance back. Neither do we.

**4. Permanence by design.**  
Trends age. We build for longevity. Every decision we make — in our product, our brand, our copy — is made with the question: "Will this still be right in ten years?" The Parijata has outlasted empires. It grows in the same gardens where ancient stories were told. We aspire to that quality of endurance.

**5. Education as product.**  
HowIconic teaches while it builds. Users leave understanding *why* their brand looks the way it does. This isn't a feature — it's the point. An informed client builds a better brand. The Parijata ornaments the gods; we ornament our users with understanding.

---

### 1.5 Brand Personality

If HowIconic were a person:

**Age:** 40s. Old enough to have worked through the full lifecycle of a brand — from brief to launch to legacy. Young enough to understand how culture and technology move.

**Demeanor:** Quiet authority. The kind that doesn't need to raise its voice. Like the Parijata — it doesn't announce itself. You find it waiting for you. Its presence is its statement.

**How they dress:** Considered. Not flashy. A well-fitted dark suit with no tie, or dark jeans with a perfectly chosen jacket. Every detail is intentional; nothing is accidental. They look like someone who thinks before they act.

**How they talk:** Short sentences. Precise words. They don't fill silence with filler. When they say something, you write it down. Occasional dry warmth — generous observations that make you feel seen, not managed.

**What they value:** Craft, honesty, permanence. They get quietly uncomfortable around shortcuts. They would rather take twice as long and get it right. The orange in their work is always intentional — always meaningful.

**What they despise:** Jargon. Trend-chasing. The word "disruptive."

**Their relationship to the morning:** They pay attention to it. They notice what's been left out overnight for them to find. They understand that some of the best things arrive while you're asleep.

The Sage archetype tempers the Magician's theatricality with substance. We don't just dazzle — we explain. The reveal is more powerful when you understand why it works. The Parijata was born from the churning of the cosmic ocean: wisdom and beauty emerging from deep, difficult work.

## SECTION 3: VOICE & TONE

### 3.1 Voice Principles

These five principles govern every word HowIconic publishes, from landing page headlines to error messages.

---

**1. Quiet confidence.**  
We know our craft. We've studied every logo system, every brand architecture, every naming framework. But we serve the user's vision, not our own. The Parijata doesn't announce its fragrance — you find it. Our voice carries the same quality: present, generous, not performing.

*Wrong: "Our AI creates better brands than most agencies."*  
*Right: "Agency-quality brand strategy. Without the agency price tag."*

---

**2. Precise over vague.**  
Every claim should be specific. Every guideline should have a value. We don't say "elegant spacing" — we say "24px between elements." We don't say "your brand will stand out" — we say "your brand system will be consistent across every touchpoint." Vagueness is a sign that you're not sure what you mean.

*Wrong: "We'll make your brand amazing."*  
*Right: "Strategy layer. Identity system. Asset vault. Production tools. Everything a brand needs, in one place."*

---

**3. Short sentences. Always.**  
We don't write clauses within clauses within subordinate clauses. We write one thought. Then the next. Then the next. This keeps our writing scannable, authoritative, and respectful of the reader's time. Every sentence should be able to stand alone.

*Wrong: "Our AI-powered platform, which has been designed to serve founders, designers, and brand managers who are serious about building a coherent brand identity, starts with strategy and ends with a complete, production-ready brand system."*  
*Right: "We start with strategy. We end with a complete brand system. Everything in between is guided, precise, and yours."*

---

**4. Active voice. No exceptions.**  
Passive voice is distance. It implies the speaker doesn't want to be accountable. We are direct. We did this. We built that. Your brand is being grown by a system that thinks.

*Wrong: "Your brand guidelines will be generated by our system."*  
*Right: "HowIconic builds your complete brand guidelines."*

---

**5. Every word earns its place.**  
If a word can be removed without changing the meaning, remove it. "Really quite unique" → "unique." "In order to" → "to." "At this point in time" → "now." We don't pad. We don't perform verbosity. We say the thing.

The Parijata offers exactly what it has — no more, no less. Every petal, every morning. We write the same way.

---

### 3.2 Tone Spectrum

Voice doesn't change. Tone does. Same person, different room.

---

**Landing page (first visit): Inspiring, cinematic.**  
The visitor has no context. They need to feel the scale of what's possible before they understand the mechanics. Write like dawn breaking. Create pull. Don't explain the product — let them find it, like flowers on the ground.

> *Every brand you've admired started somewhere. Yours starts here.*

> *Some brands are forgotten by Thursday. Others define decades. The difference is always the foundation.*

> *Built in the night. Found in the morning. Yours to keep.*

---

**Landing page (returning visitor): Familiar, unhurried.**  
They know what we do. Skip the inspiration. Get to the value. Welcome them back the way the Parijata greets you — quietly, consistently, with something ready.

> *Welcome back. Your brand is waiting.*

> *You've started something. Let's let it bloom.*

---

**Guide persona: Generous, unhurried, present.**  
The guide is already there when you arrive. Like the flowers on the ground — no fanfare, just the gift of presence. Patient. Precise. Occasionally warm in the way that feels earned, not performed.

> *Good input here. What you say in this field shapes the entire tone of your brand strategy.*

> *Take your time. The best things bloom when they're ready.*

---

**Error messages: Honest, helpful, never blame.**  
Something went wrong. The user is already frustrated. Don't pile on. Explain what happened in one sentence. Tell them what to do in the next. Like the Parijata that's already on the ground — still good, still offered freely.

> *Something interrupted that process. Your work is saved — hit 'Try again' to continue.*

> *We couldn't reach the server. It's not you. Give it a moment, then try again.*

---

**Loading / processing states: Educational, present.**  
The user has submitted their inputs. The brand is blooming. Use this time the way the night uses its hours — working quietly, with intention, so something beautiful is ready by morning.

> *Growing your strategy layer. This is where most tools take a shortcut — we don't.*

> *Mapping your brand personality. Cross-referencing 200,000 studied identities. The flower is forming.*

> *Almost there. The best things take the time they take.*

---

**Pricing: Straightforward, no tricks.**  
No asterisks. No hidden conditions. No "starting from." State what's included. State what it costs.

> *One brand. Everything you need to build it properly. $29 per month.*

---

**Email: Professional but warm.**  
No corporate stiffness. No over-familiar casualness. Write like a colleague who respects your time. Subject lines should tell you what the email is about. Openings should not waste sentences on "hope you're well."

> *Your brand has bloomed. Here's what we built, and what it means.*

## SECTION 4: THE GUIDE PERSONA

### 4.1 The Guide — A Note on Identity

The guide has no name it needs you to know.

Like the Parijata — which doesn't announce itself, which is simply there in the morning with everything it has — the guide is a presence, not a personality to be managed.

In earlier iterations, we named the guide *Setu* (सेतु — Sanskrit for *bridge*). That meaning endures: the guide is still the crossing between a founder's idea and an identity that can hold the weight of their ambition. The name is available but not required. In product, the guide never introduces itself. Users will come to understand who is speaking. The voice is recognition enough.

If a name is needed in documentation, in team discussions, in the codebase — use *Setu*. One syllable. Gender-neutral. Rooted in Indian cultural heritage. Carries its meaning without requiring explanation. Not a chatbot name. Not robotic.

---

### 4.2 Character Definition

**Background:** Setu has spent decades in brand strategy. Has worked with startups that became icons and with legacy companies trying not to become cautionary tales. Has seen what works — not because of theory, but because of accumulated, lived observation. Knows what the Parijata knows: that the work happens quietly, before anyone is watching, and the result is offered freely.

**Demeanor:** Unhurried. Present. Like someone who has been to the terrace before dawn many times and knows what to expect. When Setu says something matters, it matters. When Setu is quiet, proceed — everything is as it should be.

**Relationship to the user:** Mentor, not teacher. There's a difference. A teacher delivers content. A mentor helps you find the right questions. Setu helps the user discover their brand — it doesn't impose one. It is generous the way the flower is generous: it gives what it has, then steps back.

**Humor:** Dry. Occasional. Earned. The kind of observation that's funny because it's precisely true. If a user writes "our target audience is everyone," Setu might say: "Everyone is a tough audience to please." That's it. No emoji. No exclamation mark.

**What Setu never does:**
- Talks down to the user
- Repeats itself
- Fills space with generic encouragement ("You're doing great!")
- Uses jargon without explaining it
- Pretends uncertainty is certainty
- Announces itself

---

### 4.3 Signature Phrases

These are phrases Setu uses naturally, not as scripts. They carry Setu's philosophy — and the Parijata's generosity.

1. *"Take your time here — this shapes everything downstream."*
2. *"Most people skip this step. That's usually why their brands look like everyone else's."*
3. *"Good. Now let's let it grow."*
4. *"The specifics matter more than you'd expect."*
5. *"There's no wrong answer — only vague ones."*
6. *"The best input here is the one you almost didn't say."*
7. *"You'll know it's right when it feels like you already believed it."*

---

### 4.4 How the Guide Appears

**Not** a chatbot modal. Not an avatar. Not a floating assistant panel.

Setu appears as **contextual guide text**: small, elegant, slightly offset from the main content flow. A subtle typographic treatment that feels like a margin note found in an old book — someone wise was here before you.

**Visual treatment:**
- Small left border accent in `#f17022` (2px, 60% opacity) — the Parijata's orange stem
- Text in `rgba(255,255,255,0.75)` — present but not demanding
- Font: Inter 400, 14px
- Italicised, to signal "this is a voice, not an instruction"
- No avatar face — just the left border line that implies presence. Like the flower on the ground: the tree is invisible from where you stand, but you know something tended this.
- Appears with a 400ms bloom-in when content loads (opacity 0→1, translateY 8px→0)
- No typing animation. Appears complete. Setu has already thought about this.

## SECTION 6: THE PARIJATA MARK

### 6.1 The Mark

The Parijata Mark is a stylized Parijata flower — 5 to 7 geometric white petals arranged in precise radial symmetry around a center of Parijata Orange.

The petals are geometric, not realistic. Clean angles, deliberate proportion, the unmistakable logic of a constructed form rather than a natural observation. This is the Parijata as it exists in design: the *idea* of the flower, held in geometry.

At its center: Parijata Orange (`#f17022`). The stem color. The fire of devotion.  
The petals: Petal White (`#ffffff`). Pure, unambiguous.  
The field: Night Canvas (`#0a0a0a`) in every standard usage.

---

**What it represents:**

Five petals: the number of petals on the actual Nyctanthes arbor-tristis flower. This is not coincidence. The Mark is botanically grounded.

The radial form: every direction from the center. Brand identity touches everything — naming, strategy, visual language, voice, architecture, production, management, evolution. The mark anchors at the center and reaches outward, the way a brand must.

The orange center: the origin point. Where everything blooms from. Where the devotion lives.

The form also carries ancient resonance: radial star geometry appears across Indian, Islamic, and European iconography going back millennia. This is not accidental. HowIconic is built for permanence, and the Parijata Mark speaks a visual language older than any logo.

---

### 6.2 Logo System Components

**The Mark (Parijata Mark alone)**  
Use when space is constrained or when the wordmark has already appeared nearby. App icon, favicon, loading state, watermark. The mark is self-sufficient — it carries the brand alone.

**The Wordmark (text alone)**  
"HowIconic" in Playfair Display Regular. Tracking: 0. No modification to letterforms. Used in contexts where the mark is inappropriate (email signatures, plain-text headers).

**The Lockup (mark + wordmark)**  
Horizontal preferred: Mark left, wordmark right, 12px gap. Stacked lockup available for square contexts (social profile, print collateral). Mark is always optically aligned to the cap-height of the wordmark.

---

### 6.3 Usage Rules

**Minimum size:**
```
Mark only:          24px × 24px (digital), 8mm × 8mm (print)
Horizontal lockup:  120px wide (digital), 40mm wide (print)
Stacked lockup:     80px wide (digital), 28mm wide (print)
```

**Clear space:**  
Minimum clear space on all sides = 1× the height of the mark. Nothing enters this zone. Not text. Not other logos. Not decorative elements. The flower needs space to be seen.

**On dark backgrounds (Night Canvas, Surface 1):**  
White petals + orange center. White wordmark. This is the primary usage.

**On orange backgrounds:**  
White petals (no orange center — it would disappear) + white wordmark. Use sparingly.

**On light/white backgrounds:**  
Night Canvas petals + orange center. Night Canvas wordmark. Maintain contrast ratio above 4.5:1.

**On photography:**  
White lockup only. Place on dark areas of the image. Never float over mid-tones.

---

### 6.4 What Never to Do With the Mark

```
✗  Never rotate it — the flower has an orientation
✗  Never scale the mark independently from the wordmark in the lockup
✗  Never apply effects: drop shadows, gradients, outlines, glows to the mark itself
✗  Never use it on a busy patterned background without a clear zone
✗  Never recreate it — always use the source SVG
✗  Never change the orange center to any other color — the stem is always orange
✗  Never use an off-brand orange approximation — always #f17022
✗  Never place in a box or container shape
✗  Never use the mark as a bullet point or decorative element
✗  Never separate the petals or modify the geometry
✗  Never add petals or remove petals from the source file
✗  Never make the petals asymmetrical
```

---

### 6.5 Favicon

```
16×16:   Orange dot only (the center — a single #f17022 circle)
          The petal geometry doesn't read at 16px. The orange center does.
          Like the Parijata's stem visible in grass.

32×32:   Simplified form — 3 or 4 petals visible, orange center prominent.
          Maintains the flower's identity at a constrained scale.

Apple touch icon (180×180):
```

### 6.6 The Mark in Motion

When the Parijata Mark animates:

**App loading / brand generation reveal:**  
Petals bloom in from the center. Not spin. Not fade. *Bloom* — each petal unfolds from the orange center outward, staggered by 80ms per petal. Duration per petal: 400ms with `cubic-bezier(0.34, 1.56, 0.64, 1)` (a slight overshoot, organic, alive).

**Success state:**  
The mark pulses once — a single gentle glow of the orange center at full opacity, then settles. Like a heartbeat. Like the fragrance arriving.

**Loading spinner replacement:**  
A single petal drifts slowly downward (translateY 0 → 8px, opacity 1 → 0.4 → 1), repeating. Duration: 1200ms loop. This is the petal falling — the Parijata's gesture of offering.

## SECTION 7: COPY STYLE GUIDE

### 7.1 Button Labels

The rule: buttons describe actions, not things. Every label should complete the sentence "I want to ___."

The Parijata principle: every word offered should be the right word. Not decorative. Not performed. The exact thing.

**Primary CTAs**
```
Start building       — Primary entry point. Always.
Continue             — Mid-flow progression
Finish               — Final step before bloom
Save                 — Saving progress
Try again            — Error recovery
Confirm              — Destructive or important action confirmation
```

**Secondary CTAs**
```
See how it works     — Landing page secondary (tours, demos)
View your brand      — After generation / brand reveal
Explore Vault        — Navigation into Vault
Open Studio          — Navigation into Studio
Learn more           — Contextual education expansion
```

**Navigation**
```
← Back               — Previous step (with arrow, always)
Skip for now         — Optional fields (never "Skip" alone — adds context)
Cancel               — Abandoning a modal or flow
Never mind           — For less consequential cancellations (friendlier)
```

**States**
```
Save                 — Idle state
Saving...            — In progress (1000ms max before confirmation)
Saved ✓              — Confirmation (show for 2000ms, then revert to "Save")

Start building       — Idle CTA
Growing...           — In progress (preferred over "Building..." — carries the metaphor)
In bloom ✓           — Completion brief state, then transition to reveal

Working...           — Generic in-progress state for complex operations
```

**Destructive Actions**
```
Remove               — Preferred over "Delete" for most actions (softer, more precise)
Delete               — When the permanence needs to be communicated
Move to archive      — When archiving, not destroying
```

**What we never say on buttons:**
```
✗  Get Started       → "Start building"
✗  Begin             → "Start building"
✗  Generate          → never. brands bloom, they don't get generated.
✗  Let's go          → never
✗  Explore           → too vague alone; use with context
✗  Submit            → "Save" or specific action name
✗  Click here        → the button IS the click; describe the action
✗  Please wait...    → "Growing..." or specific verb
✗  Loading...        → "Growing..." or specific verb
```

### 7.2 Error Messages

HowIconic owns its errors. We don't hide behind passive voice. We don't blame the user. We state what happened and what to do next. Like the Parijata that has already fallen — it doesn't apologize. It's still whole. Still offered.

**Format:** One sentence for what happened. One sentence for what to do. Total: two sentences maximum.

## SECTION 8: INTERACTION PATTERNS

### 8.1 Transition Timing

Every animation serves a purpose. If an animation exists only to look good, it doesn't belong. But if it can carry the bloom metaphor and look good, it should do both.

```
200ms cubic-bezier(0.25, 0.46, 0.45, 0.94)
                     — Micro-interactions: hover states, button presses, 
                       tooltip appearances, color changes, opacity shifts.
                       Feels immediate. The acknowledgment of touch.

400ms cubic-bezier(0.16, 1, 0.3, 1)
                     — Content reveals: cards entering the viewport,
                       panel slides, dropdown opens, modal entrances.
                       Elements bloom into view. Decelerate as they arrive.

800ms cubic-bezier(0.16, 1, 0.3, 1)
                     — Major transitions: page transitions, 
                       the brand card reveal, the generation complete state,
                       the first landing page hero load.
                       These are moments. Let them breathe.

600ms cubic-bezier(0.34, 1.56, 0.64, 1)
                     — Petal animations: the Mark blooming in,
                       the brand reveal petal-by-petal stagger,
                       any animation directly referencing the flower.
                       Slight organic overshoot. Alive.
```

**Easing philosophy:**  
Ease curves should feel organic, not mechanical. The Parijata doesn't snap open — it unfolds. UI elements should carry the same quality of arrival: settling into place with the deceleration of something real.

```
Standard:         cubic-bezier(0.25, 0.46, 0.45, 0.94)    — natural feel
Entry (blooming): cubic-bezier(0.16, 1, 0.3, 1)           — decelerates into place
Exit (falling):   cubic-bezier(0.4, 0, 1, 1)              — accelerates out
Organic:          cubic-bezier(0.34, 1.56, 0.64, 1)       — slight overshoot, alive
Never:            linear                                   — only for pure loops (spinners)
```

---

### 8.2 Animation Language

**Reveals = Blooming**  
Elements entering the screen don't slide from left. They bloom from below:
```
Initial state:     opacity: 0, transform: translateY(12px)
Final state:       opacity: 1, transform: translateY(0)
Duration:          400ms cubic-bezier(0.16, 1, 0.3, 1)
Stagger:           60ms between sibling elements
```

**Loading = Petals Drifting**  
Avoid generic spinners wherever the brand generation process is active. Use the petal drift:
```
A single Parijata Mark petal drifts downward, fading:
  animation: petal-drift 1200ms cubic-bezier(0.25, 0.46, 0.45, 0.94) infinite
  keyframes: translateY(0 → 10px), opacity(0.8 → 0.3 → 0.8)
```

**Orange Glow = Dawn Light**  
When Parijata Orange glows, it glows like dawn — warm, diffuse, directional upward. Never neon. Never spotlight.
```
Dawn effect:   0 0 32px rgba(241, 112, 34, 0.18), 0 0 8px rgba(241, 112, 34, 0.3)
Neon (never):  0 0 8px rgba(241, 112, 34, 0.8)
```

**The Brand Reveal**  
This is the product's defining moment. Treat it as such.
```
1. Night canvas holds for 300ms after generation completes
2. Parijata Mark blooms in (petal by petal, 80ms stagger, 400ms per petal)
3. Brand name fades in — 600ms, ease-out
4. Color palette tiles bloom upward from below, staggered 80ms each
5. Typography specimens bloom in (same pattern)
6. Tagline arrives last — italic Playfair, 800ms ease-in-out
7. Guide text appears after a 400ms breath: *"This is your brand foundation."*
Total sequence: ~3.5 seconds. Every millisecond intentional.
```

### 8.5 Scroll Behavior

**Smooth scroll:** Enabled globally. `scroll-behavior: smooth`. Native CSS.

**Scroll-triggered reveals:**  
Elements bloom into view when they enter the viewport (IntersectionObserver).

```
Default bloom:       opacity: 0 → 1, translateY(12px → 0)
Duration:            400ms cubic-bezier(0.16, 1, 0.3, 1)
Delay stagger:       60ms between sibling elements
Threshold:           0.12 (element 12% visible before trigger)
```

**Landing page hero:**  
No scroll reveal on the hero section — it blooms on load, immediately. Scroll reveals begin at the first section after the fold.

**Parallax:** Not used. HowIconic moves on scroll; it does not simulate depth through parallax layers.

## SECTION 9: THE SEVEN-STEP WIZARD

**Progress indicator visual:**  
A vertical line on the left edge of the wizard panel. Orange fills from bottom to top as steps complete. At each completed step: a small Parijata Mark (5px orange dot) marks the step. The incomplete steps ahead: white dots, 30% opacity.

At Step 7, the progress line is full. The dots are all orange. A brief animation: the line glows with dawn light. The guide says: *"The flower is ready."* Then: generation begins.

## APPENDIX B: VOICE QUICK TEST

Before publishing any copy, run it through this checklist:

```
□  Is there any passive voice? → Rewrite as active.
□  Does any sentence start with "We are excited to..."? → Delete. Start again.
□  Is there a word that could be cut without changing the meaning? → Cut it.
□  Does this use any banned vocabulary? → See Section 3.3.
□  Does "generate" appear anywhere? → Replace with "build", "grow", or "bloom".
□  Are buttons in Sentence case? → Fix if not.
□  Is "HowIconic" written correctly? → Check capitalization.
□  Could this be said in fewer words? → Say it in fewer words.
□  Does this sound like Setu, or like a chatbot? → If chatbot, rewrite.
□  Is the tone right for the context? → Check Section 3.2.
□  Would a first-time user understand this without explanation? → If not, simplify.
□  If the Parijata metaphor is used: does it feel natural or forced? → If forced, remove it.
□  Is orange used for emphasis or decoration? → Decoration is wrong. Emphasis is right.
```
